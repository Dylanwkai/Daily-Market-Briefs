## 2. Theme Tracker

*No prior briefing — tracker initialized with the four seed themes. Deltas from tomorrow.*

### fed-path — US Fed path & inflation — TURNING (dovish)
- **What changed (24h):** This week flipped the debate. 2026's story has been a hike scare — Gov. Waller said the Fed should hike "in the near term" if CPI/PPI came in hot. They didn't: CPI slowed sharply Tuesday, PPI fell 0.3% Wednesday (core PPI 4.7% y/y vs 5.2% expected). July 28–29 hold odds now 85%+.
- **Market effect:** 2Y -4bp to 4.145%; 10Y 4.555%; equities lifted; gold holding above $4,000 — the market still doubts the long-run inflation anchor.
- **Concept:** With inflation elevated, the policy debate is hike-vs-hold, not when-to-cut. Two soft prints move the entire fed funds futures strip, and the front end of the curve (2Y) is the cleanest read on that repricing.

### hormuz-oil — Hormuz / oil shock — STABLE, elevated tail risk
- **What changed (24h):** US launched fresh strikes on Iran and reinstated a naval blockade of Iranian ports; Iran maintains it has shut the strait and has attacked tankers. Yet flows persist — 8.5m bbl transited Sunday — and Brent added just 22 cents Wednesday to $84.95, after touching ~$85.92 Tuesday.
- **Market effect:** Brent up ~10% from mid-June; European equities capped by war headlines despite strong earnings (ASML, Richemont); no panic in VIX (15.88).
- **Concept:** Oil doesn't price the headline, it prices probability-weighted barrels lost. If tankers keep moving, a "closed" strait carries a small premium — the tail scenario (a real, enforced closure removing ~20% of seaborne crude) is what would gap prices, and tails are cheap to underprice.

### ai-capex — AI capex cycle — WEAKENING / TURNING
- **What changed (24h):** Three cracks in one week: Meta is building "Meta Compute" to resell excess AI capacity; Chinese DRAM maker CXMT is preparing an IPO to fund expansion; Apple is reportedly talking to Chinese chip suppliers. Result: Micron -8% in the US, then Kospi -7%, [[SK Hynix]] -11%, Samsung -8% today. Counterpoint: [[ASML]] raised 2026 guidance on AI demand, +3.8%.
- **Market effect:** Memory complex cratered; equipment names split (ASML up, Lam down); SpaceX broke below its $135 IPO price as AI-adjacent froth deflates; Nasdaq still rose on inflation relief.
- **Concept:** Capex booms crack when buyers of capacity turn into sellers. Meta reselling compute signals capacity may exceed internal need — that compresses expected returns on everyone's capex, and the highest-beta expression of the fear is commodity memory.

### bond-regime — Bond market regime — STABLE (long end heavy)
- **What changed (24h):** Cool CPI+PPI rallied the front end hardest: 2Y 4.145% (-4bp), 10Y 4.555%, 30Y still 5.09%. The curve bull-steepened — short yields fell more than long ones.
- **Market effect:** Equities took the falling-yield lift; gold above $4,000 and a 30Y above 5% say term premium and inflation credibility remain the structural worry, not the next meeting.
- **Concept:** The front end prices the Fed's path; the long end prices long-run inflation plus term premium (extra yield for holding duration risk). When soft data rallies only the front end, the market believes the data but not the long-run anchor — that caps equity multiples even in "good news" tapes.

## 6. Idea Lab — [[SK Hynix]] (000660 KS)

*Learning exercise, not a recommendation. Chosen because it sits at the exact center of today's tape.*

**How it makes money:** The world's #2 memory maker. Two commodity businesses — DRAM (working memory) and NAND (storage) — where price is set by industry supply/demand, not by Hynix. Layered on top: [[HBM]] (high-bandwidth memory), the stacked DRAM bolted onto AI accelerators, where Hynix has been Nvidia's lead supplier. HBM behaves less like a commodity: capacity is committed to specific customers up to a year ahead, so pricing is contract-like. The three KPIs: (1) DRAM ASP — the cycle in one number; (2) HBM share of DRAM revenue and whether next year's capacity is "sold out"; (3) industry capex / bit-supply growth — today's capex is next 18 months' supply, hence CXMT's expansion plan hitting the stock.

> [!TIP]
> **Bull:** HBM is a quasi-contractual moat inside a commodity company, AI demand still compounds, and an 11% one-day markdown prices a downcycle that hasn't shown up in orders.

> [!CAUTION]
> **Bear:** Memory is entering oversupply — CXMT adds Chinese capacity, Apple legitimizes it, and if hyperscalers (see Meta) overbuilt, even "sold out" HBM gets renegotiated.

**Differentiating question:** Are Hynix's 2027 HBM supply agreements take-or-pay (customers pay even if they cut usage) or volume-flexible? That determines whether HBM revenue is genuinely contractual or commodity revenue with better PR — and almost nobody outside the supply chain knows.

## 7. Trade Floor — Two Open Tensions

**Equity — Korean memory: capitulation or first leg down?** One side: a 7% index day with a circuit breaker is classic capitulation; the AI demand signal (ASML raising guidance, TSMC's 61% AI mix) contradicts the oversupply panic; Samsung+Hynix are 60% of Kospi cap, so the move is mechanical as much as fundamental. Other side: memory cycles turn exactly like this — supply announcements (CXMT) arrive before price declines show up, and Meta reselling compute is the first demand-side confession. *Settling datapoint: TSMC's 2026 capex guide tonight — a raise refutes the overbuild thesis, a trim confirms it.*

**FICC — Is oil's Hormuz premium too small?** One side: Brent ~$85 with a "shut" strait, active US–Iran hostilities and a naval blockade is complacent — the tail (real closure hits ~20% of seaborne crude) is worth far more than a few dollars. Other side: the market prices revealed behaviour — 8.5m bbl/day still transits, both sides need oil to flow, every prior closure threat faded; OPEC spare capacity cushions the downside. *Settling datapoint: daily Hormuz transit counts and tanker war-risk insurance premia — if insurance spikes while transits fall, the premium is too small.*
